# 🚀 Senior to Tech Lead: Success Curriculum

Welcome to the **Senior to Tech Lead Transition Curriculum**. This guide is designed for high-performing Senior Engineers who want to strategically move into a leadership role where their value is defined by the **collective success of their team**.

---

## 🏗️ Phase 1: Deep Distributed Architecture & System Design
*Focus: Master the 'Why' behind massive systems.*

- **🧩 Core Concepts:**
  - **CAP Theorem & Consistency Models**: When do you prioritize Availability over Consistency? (e.g., Shopping cart vs. Bank balance).
  - **Partitioning & Sharding Strategy**: Designing systems that grow without a "Single Point of Failure" (SPF).
  - **Latency vs. Throughput**: Tuning systems for specific performance profiles.
- **🛠️ Exercises:**
  - **Write 2 ADRs**: Document a major change (like choosing a database or a caching strategy) using the [Architecture Decision Record](https://adr.github.io/) format.
  - **Conduct a Load Test**: Use tools like `Locust` or `k6` to find the breaking point of your system.
- **✅ Learning Outcome:** You can design a system that handles 10x current traffic and defend every tradeoff made.

---

## 🤝 Phase 2: High-Leverage Mentorship & Sponsorship
*Focus: Your impact = (Your Output) + (Team's Output).*

- **💡 Core Concepts:**
  - **Sponsorship vs. Mentorship**: Pushing your team for high-visibility opportunities vs. just teaching skills.
  - **1:1 Alignment**: Understanding your teammates' career goals as well as their blockers.
  - **Radical Candor**: Giving feedback that is both direct and empathetic.
- **🛠️ Exercises:**
  - **Develop a Growth Plan**: Create a 3-month skill-up plan for a junior/mid-level peer.
  - **Reverse Pair-Programming**: Sit with a colleague and let *them* drive the code while you guide the architectural thinking.
- **✅ Learning Outcome:** At least one of your peers has seen a measurable skill improvement (e.g., promoted or leading a sub-feature) because of your guidance.

---

## 📅 Phase 3: Strategic Delivery & Roadmap Management
*Focus: Bridging the gap between Product and Engineering.*

- **📂 Core Concepts:**
  - **V2MOM Framework**: Defining Vision, Values, Methods, Obstacles, and Measures for your projects.
  - **Stakeholder Management**: How to communicate a delay without losing trust.
  - **Risk Profiling**: Identifying "long-pole" tasks early in the development cycle.
- **🛠️ Exercises:**
  - **Lead a Project Pre-Mortem**: Gather the team and ask, "If this project fails in 3 months, why did it happen?" and address those 3 risks immediately.
  - **Project Breakdown**: Take a high-level Feature Request and break it into a 2-week technical roadmap.
- **✅ Learning Outcome:** You have successfully negotiated "Technical Debt" time into a sprint by proving its business value (long-term cost savings).

---

## 🚑 Phase 4: Operational Stewardship & SRE (Site Reliability)
*Focus: Building a culture of reliability.*

- **🛡️ Core Concepts:**
  - **SLOs, SLIs, and SLAs**: Measuring what matters to the customer, not just "CPU usage".
  - **DORA Metrics**: Tracking Deployment Frequency, Lead Time for Changes, Change Failure Rate, and Time to Restore Service.
  - **Automation First**: If it happens twice, automate it.
- **🛠️ Exercises:**
  - **Define SLOs**: Set up a dashboard (Grafana/Datadog) showing the real-world error rate vs. allowed budget.
  - **Lead a Blameless Post-Mortem**: Lead the analysis of a production incident without finger-pointing.
- **✅ Learning Outcome:** The team’s "On-Call Burndown" has decreased because of proactive infra improvements you spearheaded.

---

## 💬 Phase 5: Influential Leadership & Negotiation
*Focus: Leading without needing "Authority".*

- **🗣️ Core Concepts:**
  - **Decision-Making Heuristics**: Knowing when to be a "Concensus Builder" vs. a "Decisive Leader".
  - **Conflict Resolution**: Navigating technical ego and team personality clashes.
  - **Storytelling for Engineers**: Selling a technical vision to non-technical leaders.
- **🛠️ Exercises:**
  - **Propose an RFC**: Write a "Request for Comments" for a cross-team tool or process (e.g., a new testing framework) and manage the feedback loop.
  - **The No-Meeting Tuesday**: Implement a process change that improves developer "Flow State" across the entire team.
- **✅ Learning Outcome:** You are the person people come to when they have a difficult technical or interpersonal problem.

---

## 🎓 The Tech Lead Checklist (Final Self-Audit)
- [ ] Can I explain our architecture to a non-technical stakeholder?
- [ ] Have I delegated a major project to someone else successfully?
- [ ] Does my team feel safe making mistakes because of our "Blameless" culture?
- [ ] Am I spending more time thinking about 6 months from now than today's tickets?
